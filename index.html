My aspirations for the future:
Computer Science- My interest in Computer Science began with learning how software and hardware drive the digital world. Through my GCSE course, I’ve gained valuable experience in programming, problem-solving, and understanding how computer systems operate. I enjoy writing code and finding efficient solutions to challenges, and I plan to continue building on these skills through further education, such as A-level Computer Science or a technical college course. In the future, I aim to pursue a career in technology—potentially in software development, artificial intelligence, or cybersecurity. I’m excited by the fast pace of innovation in this field and the opportunity to create programs and systems that have real-world impact. I also look forward to working on personal coding projects, learning new languages, and exploring how software can solve everyday problems.
Engineering- Alongside Computer Science, I have developed a strong interest in Engineering, supported by my strengths in Maths and Physics. I’m fascinated by how physical systems, structures, and machines are designed and built. Whether it’s through understanding electrical circuits or mechanical systems, I enjoy applying scientific principles to practical, real-world scenarios. Engineering appeals to me because it combines creativity with technical skill. In the future, I’d like to explore areas such as electrical, mechanical, or robotics engineering. I’m especially interested in how technology can be used to develop sustainable solutions, improve infrastructure, and innovate in fields like transportation and energy. By continuing to study both Computer Science and Engineering, I aim to build a career where I can combine software with hardware—designing intelligent systems, building innovative products, and helping to develop the future of engineering.
